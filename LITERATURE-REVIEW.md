## 5. Gap Analysis: What Makes Infomatics Novel

### 5.1 What Exists

- **Philosophical foundation**: Wheeler (1989), Vedral (2010), QNFO Information Realism establish information as ontologically fundamental. **Well-established.**
- **Axiomatic derivations**: Chiribella et al. (2011, 2021), Hardy (2001) derive QM from information axioms. **Well-established methodology.**
- **Physics-from-information proofs**: QNFO EM proof, Mass-Energy proof show specific physics emerge from information constraints. **Partial — lack unifying framework.**

### 5.2 What Does NOT Exist — The Infomatics Gap

> NO existing work provides a **rigorous, axiomatic mathematical framework** that:
> 1. Defines information as a formal primitive (not derivative from matter/energy)
> 2. Shows ultrametric geometry as the NATURAL topology for information hierarchies
> 3. Derives physical law (QED, GR) from information-theoretic optimization
> 4. Connects to p-adic/ultrametric methods as the correct mathematics

**Novelty Score: 8/10** — The ultrametric specialization + QNFO ecosystem integration makes Infomatics genuinely novel, even though "it from bit" has a 35-year history. The specific gap — formal ultrametric information geometry — is unaddressed by any peer-reviewed work.

### 5.3 Key Citations for Phase 3 (Axiomatic Framework)

1. Chiribella, D'Ariano, Perinotti (2011) — model for axiomatic derivation
2. Information-Theoretic Reformation of Physics — explicit gap statement
3. Principia Ontologica — closest QNFO framework
