## Overview

Infomatics is a research program investigating the mathematical structure of information as the fundamental substrate of physical reality. Rather than treating information as derivative from matter, energy, or computation, Infomatics asks: what if information is *fundamental* — and matter, energy, space, and time are emergent from its structure?

## Core Thesis

> Information is not a description of reality — information IS reality. Physical systems are particular realizations of more fundamental information-theoretic structures. The laws of physics are constraints on information flow, not descriptions of independently-existing matter.

## Key Research Questions

1. **RQ-1:** What is the minimal set of information-theoretic primitives sufficient to derive physical law?
2. **RQ-2:** How does ultrametric (p-adic) geometry emerge from information-hierarchical constraints?
3. **RQ-3:** Can the Standard Model coupling constants be derived from information-theoretic optimization principles?
4. **RQ-4:** What is the relationship between information entropy and geometric curvature?
5. **RQ-5:** How does the arrow of time emerge from information-theoretic irreversibility?
